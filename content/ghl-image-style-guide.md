# GHL Ask AI — Image Style Guide (fixes "boring and plain" results)

Paste the STYLE PREFIX below at the start of every image request to GHL Ask AI —
before you describe what you actually want. Then describe the subject/scene.
This is reusable for ANY Dixon Hall image, not just gig posts.

Why your results look flat: Ask AI defaults to "safe" — centered subject, flat
even lighting, generic background, stock-photo smile. None of that is wrong,
it's just the least interesting choice every time. The fix is to remove the
"safe" options explicitly and hand it atmosphere, a real light source, and a
reason the shot exists.

---

## STYLE PREFIX (paste this every time)

```
Shoot this like a real photograph, not a rendered illustration or stock photo.

LIGHT: one clear, motivated light source — golden hour sun, a single stage
spotlight, a neon sign, a bar's Edison bulbs, headlights — never flat even
studio lighting. Let it create real shadow and falloff across the frame.

TEXTURE & ATMOSPHERE: grain, dust, haze, condensation, worn surfaces — this is
gritty Americana, not a clean product shot. Nothing should look airbrushed
or plastic-smooth.

COMPOSITION: break the center. Put the subject off to one side, shoot from a
slight low or high angle, let something cross the frame in the foreground
(a mic stand, a glass, a shoulder). A dead-center symmetrical shot is the
default to avoid.

COLOR: near-black backgrounds and warm skin tones with ONE accent color doing
the work — antique gold (#cda45f) or warm neon. Never a bright white
background, never a rainbow of competing colors.

MOOD: real, lived-in, a little rough around the edges — like a documentary
photographer caught it, not like it was staged for an ad. If it looks like it
belongs on a corporate homepage, it's wrong.

AVOID: centered symmetrical stock-photo poses, flat shadowless lighting,
plastic/airbrushed skin, glossy 3D-render look, generic bokeh-blur background
with nothing happening in it, forced smiles, stiff posture, empty negative
space, rainbow gradients, anything that reads as a template.
```

---

## Then describe your actual subject, e.g.:

```
Terry Glaze mid-solo on his Telecaster, eyes closed, lit from one side by a
warm stage light, dust visible in the beam, dark stage background,
shot slightly from below like a fan's phone photo from the front row.
```

```
John Dixon Hall Jr. backstage before a show, sitting on a road case tuning
his guitar, single bare bulb overhead, cigarette smoke or steam in the air,
candid moment — not looking at camera.
```

```
Both members walking side by side down a Fells Point cobblestone street at
dusk, guitar cases in hand, string lights and bar neon glowing behind them,
long shadows, shot from a low angle like a movie poster still.
```

## Quick reference — Dixon Hall's visual DNA
- **Palette:** near-black `#0a0705`/`#060402`, warm cream, antique gold `#cda45f`→`#e9c17e`. One accent color only.
- **Textures:** brick, grunge, worn wood, denim, leather — nothing clean or corporate.
- **Light:** always motivated (a real source in-scene), always warm, always creating shadow.
- **Reference brands for the "feel":** a Rolling Stone tour photo, a Nashville dive-bar flyer, a vintage concert poster — never a SaaS landing page or a stock photo library.

## If it's still coming out flat
Add this line at the very end of the prompt: **"This should look like it was
shot on film with a 50mm lens at night, not generated by AI."** Naming a real
camera/lens and a specific time of day forces more specific, less generic
output than describing the mood alone.
