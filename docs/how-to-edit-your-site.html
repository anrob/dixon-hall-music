How to Edit Your Website

Update your shows and music yourself — from your phone, in about a minute. No code, nothing to break.

Your website reads from one Google Sheet called “Dixon Hall — Website,” which has two tabs at the bottom: Songs and Shows. You type into a tab, the website updates itself. That’s the whole trick — it’s just a spreadsheet.

You can’t break it. You’re only typing words, dates, and links — never the design. If a box is left blank or something looks off, the site just keeps what was there before. It never goes blank or “breaks.” So go ahead and try things.

Open my website sheet →
Bookmark this. Use the two tabs at the bottom: Songs and Shows. Works in the Google Sheets app on your phone too.

1 Add a show

Open the Shows tab (bottom of the sheet) and type a new row under the last one:

  1. Venue — the place (e.g. The Roadhouse)
  2. Date — type it like 2026-08-15
  3. City — Baltimore, MD
  4. Time — 7:00 PM
  5. Ticket Link — paste a link (optional — leave blank if none)

Shows sort by date automatically, soonest first. Once a show’s date passes, it drops off the site on its own — you never have to delete old shows.

2 Post a new single

Open the Songs tab (bottom of the sheet) and type a new row:

  1. Title — the song name
  2. Status — Upcoming (turns on the countdown + Pre-Save button)
  3. Release Date — like 2026-09-01
  4. Cover — paste the cover image’s web link (URL) (I’ll set up an easy uploader for this soon)
  5. Blurb — a sentence or two about the song
  6. Pre-Save Link — paste the pre-save URL
  7. Featured — type yes so this is the one shown big on the homepage

Only one song should say yes under Featured at a time — that’s the one the site spotlights.

3 When the single comes out

The day it’s live on Spotify/Apple, open that row and:

  1. Change Status from Upcoming to Released
  2. Paste in the Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, and Amazon links you have

The countdown turns into “Listen Now” buttons automatically. Any link you leave blank just won’t show — no empty buttons.

4 Hide something (instead of deleting)

Want a song off the site but don’t want to lose it? Set its Status to Hidden. It stays in your sheet but drops off the website.

Old shows hide themselves once the date passes — nothing to do there.

How soon does it show up?

Give it up to about 5 minutes, then refresh the website. If you don’t see the change, wait a minute and refresh once more — the site checks for updates on a short timer.

Doing it from your phone

  • Install the free Google Sheets app (App Store / Google Play) and sign in with the email your invite came to.
  • Open “Dixon Hall — Website” and tap the Songs or Shows tab at the bottom.
  • Scroll to the bottom row, tap a cell, and type.
